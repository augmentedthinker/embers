## Decision

For the first real styling direction of Embers, Christopher selected **Candidate A in both columns** from the template scout artifact.

- **House style direction:** Candidate A → **Case for Astro**
- **Reader style direction:** Candidate A → **GitHub Markdown CSS**

---

## Interpretation

This is a revealing combination.

It suggests the desired surface is not primarily whimsical, ornamental, or heavily atmospheric for its own sake.
Instead, the stronger pull is toward:

- structured clarity
- inspectable thinking
- articulated reasoning
- readable documentation
- practical legibility over decorative flourish

The house-style choice leans toward a case-study logic: context, constraints, trade-offs, outcomes.
The reader-style choice leans toward familiarity and markdown-native readability.

Together, they imply a system where artifacts should feel:
- thoughtful
- grounded
- navigable
- cumulative
- credible

Rather than feeling like a dream-journal gallery, Embers may be evolving toward something like:

**a continuity archive with narrative intelligence and documented reasoning**

---

## What This Means for the Repo

The next design pass should begin aligning the repo around these choices:

### House-style direction
Use the **Case-like** direction as the main inspiration for Embers’ in-house presentation:
- more structured sections
- stronger information hierarchy
- less purely atmospheric framing
- more emphasis on the shape of thought and the logic of decisions

### Reader direction
Use the **GitHub Markdown CSS** direction as the baseline reading mode:
- simple markdown fidelity
- familiar visual language
- fast readability
- minimal friction between writing and presentation

  - **house / feature presentation mode**
